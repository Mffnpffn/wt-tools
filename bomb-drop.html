★ BOMBING TIPS
★ ACCURATE BOMBING IN WAR THUNDER
  • ★Level bombing is most accurate — fly straight and level for 3+ seconds before release so the bomb sight stabilises
  • ★Use the bomb sight (default key: F7 in cockpit view) for high-altitude precision — align the crosshair on target and wait for the indicator
  • ★For dive bombing, release between 800-1200m altitude and pull up immediately — any lower risks self-damage from your own blast
  • ★Heavier bombs have longer fuse delays — FAB-9000 and FAB-5000 need extra altitude to avoid self-kill
  • ★Wind doesn't affect bombs in War Thunder, but your aircraft's momentum carries the bomb forward — faster speed = release earlier
  • ★For base bombing, drop all bombs in one pass rather than making multiple runs — you're vulnerable during each approach
  • ★In ground RB, low-altitude bombing (200-500m) with smaller bombs is effective but requires pulling up hard after release
  • ★Use a bomb delay fuse of 1.5-2 seconds for low-altitude drops to give yourself time to escape the blast
  • ★CCRP (Continuously Computed Release Point) is available on modern jets — it automatically calculates the drop point for you
  • ★When toss-bombing, pull up at 45° and release at the top of the arc for maximum standoff distance from the target
